Hydrogen Motorcycle

This was my 2023 Capstone. The project aimed to establish the feasibility of powering a motorcycle using a hydrogen fuel cell. The project goals were to source and calculate the total cost of components for the powertrain design and to perform high-level component integration and simulation. My contributions included creating components of the CAD, designing the fuel cell schematics, and the Simulink simulation of how the fuel cell, supercapacitor, DC/DC converter, and motor system perform under different loading conditions.

Components Overview

I created a general schematic to demonstrate how each component functions within the Fuelcell system. The schematic, legend, and table describing each Fuelcell component can be found below.

Fuel Cell Stack

  • The fuel cell stack generates direct current (DC) from an electrochemical reaction
  • The fuel cell takes in hydrogen and air as fuel
  • The outputs of the fuel cell are hydrogen (recirculated), air, and water vapour

    • Hydrogen Flow

  • Hydrogen is released from the hydrogen tank, controlled by the fuel cell controller
  • The hydrogen passes through an analogue pressure gauge
  • A quick release refill valve is attached to the hydrogen circuit for refueling
  • Solenoid supply valve is used to control the flow of the hydrogen inputting to the system
  • Venturi meter is used to recirculate the output hydrogen back into the main line
  • Pressure transducer is controlled by the fuel cell controller and can control the pressure of the hydrogen
  • Solenoid supply valve is used to control the flow of the hydrogen output from the system
